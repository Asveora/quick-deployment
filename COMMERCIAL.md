## 3. Definition of Commercial Use

“Commercial Use” includes, but is not limited to:

* Integration of Asveora into proprietary software, platforms, or closed-source systems
* Use of Asveora as part of a paid service, subscription product, SaaS platform, or enterprise offering
* Redistribution of Asveora or derivative systems in a commercial context
* Embedding Asveora into products or services that generate direct or indirect revenue
* Internal corporate use where Asveora contributes operational, infrastructural, or financial value at scale
* Rebranding, white-labeling, or reselling Asveora or derivative works
* Any deployment intended primarily for organizational, institutional, or commercial advantage

If value is derived from Asveora in a structured or scalable business context, it is considered Commercial Use.

Commercial use of Asveora is not automatically granted under GPLv3 beyond its open-source terms.

Any entity engaging in Commercial Use must obtain explicit written permission and a commercial license from Asveora Social.

Asveora is not a neutral commodity software product. It is an ecosystem infrastructure project designed and maintained for public benefit.

Commercial actors are expected to:

* Respect the open-source nature and governance of the project
* Contribute financially or structurally to the Asveora ecosystem when deriving commercial value
