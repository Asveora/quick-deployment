00. Key Terms
When we say "Asveora," "we," "us," and "our" in these terms, we mean the Asveora Ecosystem, including asveora.social, its governing frameworks, and all associated Networks, Federations, Systems, Wheelcores, LandHubs, and Profile Spaces operating under this Constitution.

When we say "services," we mean all applications, websites, protocols, governance systems, tools, and civic or digital infrastructure operated within or on behalf of the Asveora Ecosystem, including those operated by Networks and Federations.

When we say "user," "citizen," "member," or "Asveoran," we mean an individual or recognized entity that participates in an Asveora Network and is assigned an Asveora Unified Identity Descriptor (AUID) for governance participation, civic interaction, and constitutional accountability.

When we say "AUID" (Asveora Unified Identity Descriptor), we mean the singular civic identity unit assigned to a human participant within the Asveora Ecosystem. Each human is constitutionally entitled to only one canonical AUID. Duplicate, legacy, fraudulent, or operationally overlapping AUIDs must consolidate into a single canonical AUID under SHCWU enforcement. The AUID is the root unit of governance participation, including voting rights, jury participation, and civic influence. All Profile Spaces, Network accounts, Communications Profiles, and civic representations associated with a human must resolve to a single AUID for purposes of identity integrity, governance weighting, and enforcement under Section 12.

When we say "you" or "your," we mean the individual or legal entity accessing or using Asveora services. If you are acting on behalf of an organization, you confirm you have authority to bind that organization, and all references to "you" or "your" apply to that entity.

When we say "External Visitors," we mean any person or entity that is not a recognized member of an Asveora Network and does not hold an AUID. External Visitors may access or interact with publicly available Asveoran spaces but do not possess governance rights, civic participation rights, or voting influence within the Ecosystem.

When we say "Network," we mean a self-governing civic structure within the Asveora Ecosystem that may contain, coordinate, or index subordinate civic units such as Wheelcores, LandHubs, Systems, and Profile Spaces. Networks operate under this Constitution, maintain independent internal governance systems, and remain fully bound to Ecosystem-wide rules, including Civic Identity Integrity (Section 12) and Federation governance constraints.

When we say "Federation," we mean a formally declared alliance between two or more Networks within the Asveora Ecosystem. Federations exist only through explicit governance declaration and operate under structured entry, exit, and transition rules, including mandatory cooldown periods. Federations do not alter or multiply civic identity, as all governance influence remains bound to a single AUID per human being.

When we say "Civic Identity Integrity Layer," we mean the constitutional rule system defined in Section 12 that enforces one-to-one mapping between a human being and a Civic Identity Unit (AUID), ensuring that identity, governance participation, and civic influence cannot be duplicated, fragmented, or artificially multiplied across Profiles, Networks, or Federations.

01. Preamble
Asveora is a decentralized civic Ecosystem structured as a Decentralized Democratic Constitutional Republic (DDCR), designed to enable distributed governance, civic participation, and technological self-sustainability across interconnected Networks. Its purpose is to reduce dependency on centralized platforms while enabling structured digital governance and authenticated civic interaction.

 The Asveora Constitution governs participation across the Ecosystem, including the asveora.social Network and all associated Networks, Federations, Systems, and civic infrastructures. It defines binding rules, rights, obligations, and identity constraints governing civic participation, including voting, jury participation, and governance processes anchored through the AUID (Asveora Unified Identity Descriptor) framework defined in Section 00 and enforced through identity integrity rules in Section 12.

 The Asveora Ecosystem is structured as a layered civic topology consisting of Profile Spaces, Wheelcores, LandHubs, Systems, Networks, and Federations. Each layer operates as an interoperable civic unit with distinct governance functions. Profile Spaces represent identity-facing participation layers, Wheelcores, LandHubs, and Systems function as modular operational or organizational structures, Networks serve as primary governance domains, and Federations represent formal, explicitly declared alliances between Networks governed by structured entry, exit, and transition rules.

 This Constitution was originally established by the first declared Federation of Asveora known as “Asveora Genesis.” It is maintained as a living civic document subject to structured amendment through Ecosystem governance processes. Structural provisions may remain persistent where required to preserve identity integrity, system stability, or compliance with applicable external legal frameworks.

 Participation in Asveora constitutes agreement to all principles, governance structures, identity constraints, and enforcement mechanisms defined within this Constitution, including Civic Identity Integrity (Section 12), Federation governance rules (Section 13), and Ecosystem-wide enforcement boundaries.
02. Age of Citizenship
To become an Asveoran (citizen of the Asveora Ecosystem), you must be at least 16 years of age and meet the minimum age of digital consent required in your jurisdiction. Where applicable law sets a higher minimum age, that higher requirement prevails without exception. The 16-year baseline applies only where it does not conflict with applicable legal requirements or stricter Network, Federation, System, Wheelcore, LandHub, or Profile Space policies.

Individual Networks, Federations, Systems, Wheelcores, LandHubs, and Profile Spaces may impose stricter age or eligibility requirements at their discretion. Participants are responsible for verifying and complying with all applicable governance-layer rules before participation.

If a participant is under the age of majority in their jurisdiction but meets the minimum threshold, participation is only permitted with verifiable consent from a parent or legal guardian. That guardian assumes full responsibility for compliance with this Constitution and all actions performed under associated AUID(s).

By using Asveora, you affirm and represent the following:

01. You meet or exceed all applicable age requirements under jurisdictional and governance-layer rules.
02. You are legally permitted to enter into this agreement, or have obtained valid guardian consent where required.
03. You are not prohibited from participation under applicable law or Ecosystem enforcement action.
04. You are not currently suspended, restricted, or excluded from any Network, Federation, or Ecosystem function.
05. If acting on behalf of an entity, you have authority to bind that entity to this Constitution.

Age-based safety and content restrictions may vary across Networks and Federations. Participants must comply with the strictest applicable standard in any environment they enter.

Serious violations involving minors, including exploitation, grooming, coercion, or exposure to Restricted Contexts, are strictly prohibited and subject to immediate enforcement under Network or Federation governance and applicable law.

Asveora is a decentralized civic Ecosystem that distributes operational responsibility across independent services, protocols, and infrastructure layers. Participation may involve external systems including identity providers, communication protocols, hosting services, storage systems, and federation-linked infrastructure.

By using Asveora, you acknowledge that you are solely responsible for compliance with the terms, policies, and legal requirements of any external services you choose to use in connection with the Ecosystem. This includes identity systems, communication protocols, and decentralized infrastructure (including Matrix or equivalent systems).

All Networks, Federations, Systems, Wheelcores, LandHubs, and Profile Spaces operate under independent governance rules, provided they do not conflict with this Constitution. Participation constitutes acceptance of those internal rules. All such structures must explicitly recognize this Constitution as the supreme Ecosystem governance framework.
Civic Safety & Mandatory Reporting Duties
As a condition of participation, all Asveorans are required to uphold civic safety standards protecting minors and vulnerable individuals across all Ecosystem structures.

Mandatory civic safety obligations activate when behavior meets the Suspicion Threshold Standard or indicates exploitation, grooming, coercion, or exposure to Restricted Contexts.

Required actions include:

01. Preventing continued exposure where possible
02. Refusing engagement in prohibited or exploitative interaction
03. Preserving relevant evidence when safe
04. Reporting through designated Network or Federation channels
05. Escalating to external authorities where required by law

These obligations apply uniformly across all Networks and Federations. Failure to comply, including concealment or facilitation, constitutes a severe violation of this Constitution.
Definition of Restricted Contexts
“Restricted Contexts” refers to any communication, content, interaction, or directed behavior that is legally or developmentally inappropriate for individuals under the minimum age threshold defined in Section 2.

Restricted Contexts include but are not limited to:

01. Sexual content, sexual conversations, sexual roleplay, or sexualized interaction
02. Explicit or implicit sexual material in any format, including AI-generated media
03. Content involving drugs, alcohol, or other age-restricted substances or behaviors
04. Facilitation, instruction, or encouragement of illegal or age-restricted activity
05. Grooming, coercion, or attempts to establish inappropriate relationships
06. Routing or directing minors to external adult-only spaces or content
07. Any content classified as “Adult Only” under applicable law or widely recognized platform safety standards

This definition applies across all Networks, Federations, Systems, Wheelcores, LandHubs, and Profile Spaces. No internal governance structure may weaken, reinterpret, or override this standard.
Suspicion Threshold Standard
A “reasonable belief” or “reasonable suspicion” is established when an Asveoran, acting in good faith, observes behavior or receives credible information that would lead a reasonable person to conclude that a violation of age restrictions or Restricted Context rules may be occurring.

Indicators may include but are not limited to:

01. Self-disclosed or inconsistent age information suggesting underage status
02. Behavioral patterns consistent with minor access to restricted spaces
03. Direct engagement in or request for Restricted Contexts
04. Credible third-party reports from other Asveorans
05. Attempts to conceal identity or evade age verification in suspicious contexts

Suspicion does not require definitive proof. It must, however, be based on observable behavior or credible reporting rather than speculation, bias, or abuse of reporting systems.

Once triggered, Civic Safety obligations become active immediately, including restriction from prohibited engagement and mandatory reporting.
Mandatory Reporting Channel Requirement
Every Network must maintain at least one clearly designated civic safety reporting channel within each governance or coordination hub. This may include support systems, moderation queues, designated reporting interfaces, or explicitly labeled reporting channels, provided the function is clearly identifiable.

This requirement is mandatory and non-optional. Each Network, Federation, System, Wheelcore, LandHub, and Profile Space must ensure reporting access is continuously available and operational.

This system exists to guarantee consistent enforcement of Civic Safety obligations and ensure reliable escalation pathways for Restricted Context violations and related safety concerns.
Civic Escalation and Enforcement Hierarchy
Civic safety concerns follow a strict escalation chain:

01. Local structure (Profile Space, Wheelcore, LandHub, System)
02. Network administration
03. Federation governance (if applicable)
04. External legal authorities where required by law or severity

Networks within Federations must notify Federation governance when escalation thresholds are met. Networks not part of a Federation escalate directly to external authorities where legally required.

No Network or Federation may suppress, obstruct, or refuse escalation of credible civic safety concerns requiring external reporting.
Civic Reporting Standards and Evidentiary Requirements
All reports submitted for civic safety review must include, where reasonably available, verifiable supporting evidence such as screenshots, video or audio recordings, message logs, system identifiers, timestamps, or platform references.

Reports must include a structured description of events and a coherent timeline. Submissions lacking meaningful context or evidentiary support are not eligible for formal civic escalation.

Unsupported accusations, single-sentence claims, or intentionally vague reports do not meet minimum civic reporting standards and will not be processed as valid escalation requests.

This standard exists to preserve integrity of enforcement systems and prevent abuse of civic reporting mechanisms.
Reporting System Integrity and Abuse Classification
False reporting, malicious reporting, or coordinated abuse of civic reporting systems is strictly prohibited.

Such behavior is classified as Disruptive Activity under Section 8 and Malicious Activity under Section 16 when it is used to harass, manipulate governance systems, or interfere with legitimate enforcement processes.

This includes knowingly false claims, coordinated report flooding, weaponized reporting campaigns, or misuse of safety infrastructure for retaliation or sabotage.

Reporting systems exist exclusively for good-faith civic safety escalation.
04. Privacy & Security
Asveora operates as a decentralized civic ecosystem built on Networks, Federations, and independent infrastructure layers. Privacy and security within Asveora are not enforced through a single centralized data authority, but through structural design principles, identity integrity constraints, and user-controlled participation across systems.

At the core of this model is the AUID (Asveora Unified Identity Descriptor), which represents a civic identity unit used for governance participation, voting weight, and identity resolution within and across Networks. AUID integrity is protected through ecosystem-wide rules defined in this Constitution, including identity consolidation, anti-duplication enforcement, and Federation-level coordination standards.

Privacy in Asveora is primarily determined by the Network, Federation, or external protocol you choose to interact with. Each layer of the Ecosystem may implement its own data handling, storage, and communication policies. Asveorans are responsible for understanding the privacy and security implications of any external service, protocol, or infrastructure they use in connection with the Ecosystem.

While Asveora establishes civic standards for safety and integrity, it does not rely on unnecessary centralized surveillance or mandatory data aggregation for participation. However, users should always assume that any digital communication system may involve third-party logging, routing, or storage depending on the underlying infrastructure they choose to use.

Core Security and Operational Safety Principles

01. Use strong, unique authentication credentials for all systems connected to Asveora participation.
02. Never share authentication credentials, private keys, or account access with others.
03. Store sensitive credentials in secure, encrypted environments only.
04. Treat all external links, services, and protocols as untrusted until verified.
05. Verify HTTPS encryption and domain authenticity before entering sensitive data.
06. Avoid interacting with suspicious or shortened links without validation.
07. Do not disclose personal identifiers such as addresses, financial data, or device identifiers unless absolutely required and trusted.
08. Use multi-factor authentication wherever available.
09. Avoid unsecured or unknown public network infrastructure when handling sensitive activity.
10. Maintain awareness of how personal data may be processed across Networks, Federations, and external systems.

Identity Protection and AUID Security

AUIDs are civic identity units and must not be shared, transferred, or impersonated outside of authorized identity rules defined in Section 12. Any attempt to duplicate, spoof, or improperly associate an AUID is considered a violation of Civic Identity Integrity and may result in enforcement action at the Network or Federation level.

External Systems and Third-Party Responsibility

Asveora interacts with external protocols, platforms, and infrastructure that are not controlled by the Ecosystem. These systems operate under their own privacy and security policies. Asveorans are responsible for reviewing and complying with those policies when engaging with external tools or services.

Impersonation and Security Misrepresentation Warning

No legitimate Network, Federation, or Ecosystem authority will request sensitive personal information such as passwords, private keys, financial details, government IDs, or device-level identifiers. Any request for such information should be treated as malicious and reported through the appropriate civic reporting channels defined within the relevant Network or Federation structure.

Security in Asveora is a shared responsibility between system design and user behavior, and all participants are expected to act with caution, awareness, and respect for identity integrity principles.

Asveora exists as a collectively maintained system, and its accessibility is preserved through its members rather than external commercialization.
06. Data Ownership, DMCA Procedures, and Protections
Within the Asveora Ecosystem, Asveorans retain ownership and primary control over their own creations, communications, and data as they exist within the scope of the tools, Networks, and external services they choose to use. No centralized authority within Asveora claims unilateral ownership of user-generated content or personal data.

All participation in Asveora is mediated through decentralized infrastructure layers, including Networks, Federations, Systems, Wheelcores, and LandHubs. Each of these may utilize independent hosting, storage, or communication services governed by their own operational policies. Asveorans are responsible for understanding how their data is handled at each layer they choose to engage with.

The AUID (Asveora Unified Identity Descriptor) serves as the civic identity unit used for governance participation and identity integrity across the Ecosystem. While AUID-linked activity may be indexed or referenced for civic functionality, ownership of content and data remains with the originating Asveoran subject to the rules of the relevant Network or Federation.

Asveora does not sell user data and does not operate as a centralized data brokerage system. However, content shared through external infrastructure or third-party services remains subject to the privacy and storage policies of those systems.

Asveora operates in compliance with applicable intermediary liability frameworks, including Section 230 of United States law, where relevant, and functions primarily as a civic and indexing layer rather than a centralized content publisher.

Networks and Federations may index or reference user-generated content for civic, organizational, or governance purposes. Where such content is alleged to infringe intellectual property rights, a valid DMCA notice may be submitted for review and processing by the relevant Network or Ecosystem-level reporting channels.

Ownership of data, infrastructure, repositories, systems, or operational resources does not inherently grant governance indexing authority, constitutional authority, SHCWU allocation rights, or civic control over Networks, Federations, or Ecosystem governance processes.
DMCA Notice Requirements

A valid copyright infringement notice must include sufficient information to allow identification, verification, and processing of the claim. Required elements include:

01. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent
02. Identification of the original copyrighted work
03. Identification of the allegedly infringing material (including up to 10 direct URLs where applicable)
04. Contact information including full name, address, telephone number, and email
05. A good faith statement that the disputed use is not authorized
06. A statement under penalty of perjury that the information is accurate and the claimant is authorized to act
07. Supporting documentation verifying ownership or authorization
08. A valid organizational or domain-based email address where applicable
09. Submission through the official reporting channel designated by the relevant Network or Federation

Submissions that are incomplete, abusive, or outside recognized reporting channels may not be processed. Only claims submitted through valid civic reporting infrastructure are eligible for review.

Asveora does not process personal disputes or informal requests under DMCA procedures; notices must originate from rights holders or authorized agents acting in an official capacity.

08. Prohibition of Disruptive Organization
Asveorans may not engage in, promote, coordinate, or facilitate organizational structures or coordinated activities that are intended to destabilize civic integrity, manipulate governance outcomes, or generate harm within a Network or Federation environment.

This includes but is not limited to brigading, astroturfing, coordinated harassment campaigns, political gladiatorism, reaction bait structures, recruitment or deployment of internet-based attack groups, unsolicited mass coordination efforts, shock-based mobilization tactics, flash mobs intended to disrupt civic order, and any coordinated action designed to overwhelm or distort normal civic participation.

The use of Asveora infrastructure to incite unrest, simulate warfare between entities, coordinate raids (whether digital or physical), execute raids, or facilitate cross-network disruption is strictly prohibited. This applies equally to symbolic, ironic, or “parody” constructions when their functional effect results in coordinated harm or destabilization.

Prohibited organizational forms also include communities or structures that exist primarily to manufacture harassment, amplify outrage cycles, generate harassment targets, or create environments of sustained psychological distress. This includes so-called drama hubs, harassment collectives, and systems designed to bypass normal moderation or civic accountability processes.

Doxxing in any form is strictly prohibited, including the aggregation, correlation, or repackaging of publicly available data for malicious or coercive intent.

Enforcement of this section is evaluated within the context of Civic Identity Integrity rules, including the principle that one human being corresponds to one civic influence unit per Network or Federation context. Coordinated multi-AUID influence manipulation may be treated as an aggravating factor under governance review processes.

Nothing in this section restricts legitimate civic discussion, disagreement, protest, or democratic participation conducted within the bounds of due process, transparency, and non-coercive behavior.

Where ambiguity, misuse, or potential identity manipulation is identified, governance or moderation bodies within the relevant Network or Federation may require modification, suspension, or regeneration of either the username or PIK to preserve civic clarity, trust, and system integrity.

Individual Networks and Federations may implement additional content moderation rules consistent with their governance structures. Such rules must remain aligned with Ecosystem-wide principles of civic integrity, safety, transparency, and anti-manipulation standards. Shared Civic Responsibility
Asveora encourages Asveorans to act with care, accuracy, and respect when sharing content, recognizing that shared information contributes to the broader civic and cultural environment of the Ecosystem. While enforcement is governed through structured civic systems, maintaining trust, clarity, and informational integrity is a shared responsibility of all participants.
11. Democratic Rights and Network Removals
Asveora is built upon the model of a Decentralized Democratic Constitutional Republic (DDCR). This means that executive direction within the Asveora Ecosystem is determined through political discourse and the voting rights of Asveorans within their respective Networks, including wheelcores, landhubs, systems, and Federations. Democratic participation is a core civic right granted to Asveorans as members of the networked society.

Network, Federation, and Ecosystem Authority Hierarchy
The Asveora Ecosystem operates as a layered governance structure with clearly defined authority domains. The Ecosystem represents the highest conceptual and infrastructural layer and contains all Networks, Federations, and subordinate systems. Networks function as the primary civic jurisdiction where most governance, participation, and enforcement activities occur. Federations are formal governance unions between Networks that establish unified civic rules, shared governance contexts, and coordinated enforcement standards across all participating Networks. Wheelcores, landhubs, systems, and other substructures operate under the authority of their parent Network unless explicitly redefined by Federation-level agreements. Authority for governance enforcement flows downward through these layers, while civic legitimacy and democratic authority flow upward from individual Asveorans through their respective Networks and Federations.

Asveorans have the right to vote on all eligible and properly constituted governance matters within Asveora. External Visitors do not possess voting rights, as they are not members or citizens of the Asveora Network. No person or entity within Asveora may cast a vote, represent, or exercise governance influence on behalf of any External Visitor.

Voting power within Asveora is governed by the principle that one human being corresponds to one civic influence unit within any given Network or Federation governance domain, as defined under Civic Identity Integrity rules. When Networks operate independently, this principle applies per Network. When Networks are part of a Federation, governance contexts are unified under Federation civic integrity rules, ensuring that no individual human may accumulate additional civic influence through multiple AUID representations within the same governance domain.

If an issue arises within Asveora, including but not limited to Terms of Service violations, harassment, fraud, or other forms of misconduct, an Asveoran may file a formal complaint within the designated civic coordination and governance hub of the relevant Network (for the asveora.social Network this is the Asveora Network Plaza Stoat server which can be found here) in which the issue occurred. Depending on the nature of the issue, they may also be advised to report it to the relevant platform provider or infrastructure host.

Filing a complaint may initiate a formal civic review process. Every Asveoran is entitled to due process, and any administrative or disciplinary action must follow the outcome of a structured review or court proceeding where applicable.

If an Asveoran is summoned to court, they are required to appear within the timeframe specified in the summons. A court summons constitutes a mandatory civic directive and applies only to members of the issuing Network. Failure to appear (FTA) will result in a designated grace period after which additional administrative action may be taken, including potential network restrictions or removal. Enforcement authority does not extend outside Network boundaries except where explicitly governed by Federation agreements.

Court proceedings exist to ensure fairness, accountability, and accurate assessment of reported issues. These proceedings may involve participation from relevant assemblies within Asveora and are resolved through a composite jury model intended to preserve democratic legitimacy. In urgent or high-severity cases, proceedings may be expedited under the authority of designated governance assemblies to maintain network safety and integrity.

The governing systems of Asveora are separate from the legal systems of real-world jurisdictions. A breach of Asveora’s Constitution may result in removal from the network without implying violation of real-world law. However, if conduct within Asveora involves violations of applicable local or international law, Asveora may cooperate with appropriate authorities where required.

The democratic right to vote may be temporarily suspended during active trials or civic review processes. Network removal is considered a rare outcome and is determined through network-wide governance review. Appeals may be submitted following established procedures, though resolution timelines may vary.

Removal from Asveora does not prevent independent deployment of the Asveora framework, as the project remains open source. However, such individuals or entities will no longer have access to the asveora.social ecosystem or its associated governance structures.

External Visitors cannot be summoned under any circumstances, as they do not hold civic membership within Asveora. Therefore, Asveora does not exercise direct governance authority over them. If an External Visitor violates Asveora’s Terms of Service, the responsibility for enforcement lies with the Asveoran hosting or enabling access within that space. Violations of external platform policies must be reported to the relevant platform or infrastructure provider responsible for moderation and trust enforcement.
12. Civic Identity Integrity, AUID Ownership, and Voting Consolidation
Core Civic Identity Principle
The Asveora Ecosystem operates under a strict one-human, one-civic-weight principle. Each human participant is represented through a Single Human Civic Weight Unit (SHCWU) known as an AUID (Asveora Unified Identity Descriptor). Civic influence is derived exclusively from this singular human identity mapping, regardless of the number of Profiles, Networks, or operational presences associated with that individual.

Identity Resolution Rule (Canonical Standard)
A human may operate multiple Profiles, Communications Profiles, or Network presences; however, all must resolve to a single AUID for all governance functions. Where multiple AUIDs are determined—through verification, adjudication, behavioral linkage, or disclosure—to represent the same human, they must be consolidated into a single civic identity unit across all Networks and Federations.

Voting Consolidation Principle
Civic influence, voting weight, and governance participation are computed strictly at the level of the human, not the account or profile. Any attempt to fragment, duplicate, or multiply civic influence is invalid and subject to enforcement.

AUID Integrity and Anti-Duplication Rule
No individual may maintain multiple AUIDs for the purpose of increasing civic influence or bypassing governance constraints. Detected violations must result in consolidation of identity and may trigger additional enforcement actions proportional to severity and intent.

Governance Scope Enforcement
All Networks and Federations are required to enforce AUID resolution rules within their jurisdiction. Civic identity fragmentation is prohibited across all governance scopes.

Communications Profile Separation Principle
AUIDs represent civic identity only. Communications Profiles and operational systems may exist independently but must always resolve back to a single AUID for governance actions.
13. Federation Membership Integrity, Transition Windows, and Participation Cooldowns
Federation Definition and Formal Structure
A Federation is a formally declared alliance between two or more Networks within the Asveora Ecosystem. Federations do not exist through informal association, implied coordination, or behavioral alignment. They exist only through explicit governance declaration and are dissolved only through formal recorded termination.

Mandatory Formation and Dissolution Validation
All Federation lifecycle changes, including creation, modification, and dissolution, require full governance validation and system-wide recording. No Federation state change is valid until confirmed and entered into the official civic record.

Identity Resolution Compliance (Federation Scope)
All Federation-level governance must comply with Section 12. Civic influence must resolve to a single human per SHCWU. Where duplication is detected, consolidation is mandatory across all Federation governance functions including voting, jury participation, and representation limits.

Transition Windows and Governance Stability
When a Network enters or exits a Federation, a defined transition window of seven (7) days applies during which governance state remains stable and protected. This prevents manipulation of active votes, ongoing proceedings, or real-time governance outcomes.

Exit Cooldown and Re-Entry Restriction
After exiting a Federation, a Network must observe a mandatory cooldown period of thirty (30) days before joining or rejoining any Federation. This prevents rapid cycling or strategic instability.

Re-Entry Review Requirement
Following cooldown, re-entry into a Federation may require governance review to ensure alignment with Ecosystem stability standards.

Separation from External Legal Systems
Federations exist exclusively within the Asveora Ecosystem and do not create or imply external legal jurisdiction.
14. Accessing Protocols & Staying Safe
General Protocol Access and Network Neutrality
The Asveora Ecosystem operates across multiple communication protocols, verification systems, and community coordination environments depending on Network or Federation configuration. No single protocol defines or contains the full scope of Asveora communication or civic infrastructure.

External Protocol Awareness (Including Decentralized Systems)
Decentralized communication protocols, including Matrix-based systems and similar federated networks, may be used within or alongside Asveora Networks. These systems provide flexibility and interoperability but may also increase exposure to unverified or malicious actors due to open participation structures. Users must exercise caution when interacting with unknown identities, rooms, or services.

Generalized Safety Principle
Risks such as impersonation, fraud, social engineering, and malicious coordination exist across all communication protocols, not only decentralized systems. While heightened caution is recommended in open or federated environments, all platforms require consistent vigilance and verification practices.

Identity Resolution Rule (Cross-Protocol Consistency)
All identity interactions across any protocol must ultimately resolve to a single AUID under Section 12. Protocol-specific accounts, communication handles, or external identities do not constitute separate civic identities and cannot be used to increase, duplicate, or fragment civic influence within the Ecosystem.

Trust and Verification Boundaries
Asveorans should rely on official Network or Federation verification systems when confirming identity prior to engaging in sensitive communication, governance activity, or civic coordination. Unverified or unexpected contact should be treated as potentially unsafe until validated through recognized verification channels.

Security and Impersonation Warnings
No legitimate representative of Asveora will request sensitive personal information through informal or unsolicited communication channels. Any such request should be treated as impersonation and reported immediately through appropriate governance or support systems.

Incident Reporting
Suspicious activity must be reported through the appropriate Network or Federation governance channels, and where applicable, through protocol-native reporting tools. Reports should include sufficient context and evidence to enable verification and response.
15. Transfer & Backup of Communications Profile
Core Principle of Profile Integrity
A Communications Profile within Asveora represents an operational layer of an Asveoran’s presence within a Network or Federation. In alignment with Section 12 (Civic Identity Integrity, AUID Ownership, and Voting Consolidation), all Communications Profiles must ultimately resolve to a single Civic Identity Unit (AUID) for all governance functions, including voting, jury participation, and civic influence calculations.

Multiple Profiles and Administrative Boundaries
An Asveoran may maintain multiple Communications Profiles across Networks, Systems, or protocols for functional, operational, or organizational purposes. However, these profiles do not constitute independent civic identities. All profiles must remain permanently linked to a single AUID and must not be used to bypass identity consolidation rules, governance limits, or civic participation constraints defined in Section 12.

Transfer Conditions and Identity Continuity
Communications Profiles may be transferred between devices, systems, or Networks where identity continuity is preserved. A transfer does not create, duplicate, or fragment civic identity. Any attempt to use transfer mechanisms to split identity, multiply governance influence, or evade civic constraints is a violation of Section 12 and is subject to enforcement action.

Backup Requirements and Recovery Integrity
Asveorans are responsible for maintaining secure backups of their Communications Profiles. Backup systems must preserve access continuity without enabling duplication of civic identity. Loss of access does not result in loss of AUID, and recovery processes must restore the original identity rather than generate new civic entities.

Cross-Network and Federation Handling
Within Federations, Communications Profiles may be recognized across member Networks for continuity and interoperability. However, all cross-Network recognition remains subject to Section 13 Federation Integrity rules and Section 12 identity consolidation requirements. No Federation or Network may interpret profile transfer as creating additional civic influence units.

Security, Misuse Prevention, and Enforcement
Any attempt to exploit profile transfer, backup, or recovery systems to create artificial identities, duplicate civic participation, or bypass governance limitations is strictly prohibited. Violations may result in identity consolidation enforcement, civic review, suspension of participation privileges, or other governance actions under applicable Network or Federation procedures.
16. Malicious Links, Malware, & Attacks
Core Security Principle
The Asveora Ecosystem prohibits any action intended to compromise the security, integrity, identity, or functionality of users, systems, Networks, Federations, or associated infrastructure. This includes the creation, distribution, facilitation, or execution of malicious software, exploitative code, or harmful digital activity of any kind.

Prohibited Malicious Activities
Strictly prohibited behaviors include, but are not limited to: phishing attempts, credential harvesting, IP tracking or extraction for malicious purposes, ransomware deployment, cryptojacking, man-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks, cross-site scripting (XSS), cross-site request forgery (CSRF), SQL injection, remote code execution exploits, unauthorized surveillance or interception, social engineering intended to extract sensitive data, and any other form of digital intrusion, manipulation, or system compromise.

Identity and Civic Integrity Protection
Any attempt to use malicious tools, exploits, or attacks to interfere with AUID integrity, Communications Profiles, voting systems, or identity resolution mechanisms defined in Section 12 (Civic Identity Integrity, AUID Ownership, and Voting Consolidation) constitutes a direct violation of Ecosystem civic security principles. Such actions may result in immediate restriction from governance participation systems and related civic functions.

Cross-Network and Federation Scope
Malicious activity is strictly prohibited across all Networks and Federations within the Asveora Ecosystem. Enforcement applies within each Network or Federation according to their governance structures. Where Federations exist, coordinated enforcement actions may be executed under agreed Federation security protocols. No Network or Federation may authorize, ignore, or normalize malicious activity under any circumstance.

Reporting and Response
Asveorans are encouraged to report suspected malicious activity through their respective Network’s designated civic reporting channels. Depending on severity, incidents may trigger technical mitigation, civic review, or disciplinary processes, including suspension or removal from Network participation.

Enforcement and Consequences
Violations of this section constitute severe breaches of Ecosystem trust and security. Consequences may include immediate loss of access to affected systems, civic participation restrictions, Network-level bans, or Federation-level enforcement actions. Repeated or coordinated malicious activity may be escalated to full Ecosystem governance review procedures.

Any attempt to disguise external commercial systems as internal civic infrastructure, or to route users into misleading or impersonated Ecosystem spaces for commercial gain, is considered a violation of Civic Integrity rules and may trigger reporting and enforcement procedures under Section 16 and Section 8.

Asveorans who wish to display contributor or operational badges are encouraged to participate in the Ecosystem through legitimate contribution pathways, including development, moderation assistance, community support, or other recognized civic roles. Engagement in these pathways ensures that identity signals remain transparent, earned, and aligned with Ecosystem trust standards.

Enforcement and Legal Separation
